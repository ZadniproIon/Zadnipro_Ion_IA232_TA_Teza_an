Epipelagic representations and rigid local systems

By Zhiwei Yun

Abstract

We construct automorphic representations for quasi-split groups $G$ over the function field $F=k(t)$ one of whose local components is an epipelagic representation in the sense of Reeder and Yu. We also construct the attached Galois representations under the Langlands correspondence. These Galois representations give new classes of conjecturally rigid, wildly ramified ${\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}$ -local systems over $\mathbb{P}^{1}-\{0,\infty\}$ that generalize the Kloosterman sheaves constructed earlier by Heinloth, Ngô and the author. We study the monodromy of these local systems and compute all examples when $G$ is a classical group.

1. Introduction

Let $G$ be a reductive quasi-split group over a local field $K$ as in § 1.4.4 . Recently, Reeder and Yu [18] constructed a family of supercuspidal representations of $p$ -adic groups called epipelagic representations , generalizing the simple supercuspidals constructed earlier by Gross and Reeder [9] . These are supercuspidal representations constructed by compactly inducing certain characters of the pro- $p$ part of a parahoric subgroup ${\mathbf{P}}$ of $G$ . The construction of epipelagic representations uses the $\theta$ -groups $(L_{{\mathbf{P}}},V_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ studied by Vinberg et al .

The main results of this paper include

Realization of epipelagic representations (for the local function field) as a local component of an automorphic representation $\pi$ of $G(\mathbb{A}_{F})$ , where $F$ is the function field $k(t)$ . This is done in Proposition 2.7 . Here $\pi=\pi(\chi,\phi)$ depends on two parameters $\chi$ and $\phi$ (a multiplicative on $\widetilde{L}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(k)$ and a stable linear functional $\phi:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to k$ ).

Construction of the Galois representation $\rho_{\pi}:\textup{Gal}(F^{s}/F)\to{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell})$ attached to $\pi=\pi(\chi,\phi)$ under the Langlands correspondence. This is done in Theorem 3.5 and Corollary 3.7 . The Galois representation $\rho_{\pi}$ can be equivalently thought of as an $\ell$ -adic ${\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}$ -local systems $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ over $\mathbb{P}^{1}-\{0,\infty\}$ , where ${\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}$ is the Langlands dual group of $G$ . We also offer a way to calculate these ${\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}$ -local systems in terms of the Fourier transform (see Proposition 3.9 ).

Description of the local monodromy the local systems $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ . The main result is Theorem 4.7 that describes the monodromy of $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ at $0$ when $G$ is split and $\chi=1$ . We also conditionally deduce the cohomological rigidity of $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ (see § 5 ), and make predictions on the monodromy at $0$ in general (see § 4.9 ).

Computation of the local systems $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ when $G$ is a classical group (§ 6 , § 7 and § 8 ). In each of these cases, we express the local system $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ as the Fourier transform of the direct image complex of an explicit morphism to $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . As a result, we obtain new families of exponential sums (indexed by $G$ and ${\mathbf{P}}$ ) generalizing Kloosterman sums, see Corollaries 6.6 , 7.6 and 8.7 .

In [10] , Heinloth, Ngô and the author considered the case of “simple supercuspidals” of Gross and Reeder. These correspond to the special case ${\mathbf{P}}=\mathbf{I}$ is an Iwahori subgroup. In [10] we constructed the Kloosterman sheaves $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},\mathbf{I}}(\chi,\phi)$ , and proved properties of their local and global monodromy expected by Frenkel and Gross in [7] for split $G$ .

The general case to be considered in this article exhibits certain interesting phenomena which are not seen in the special case treated in [10] .

First, the $\widehat{G}$ -local systems $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ on $\mathbb{P}^{1}-\{0,\infty\}$ form an algebraic family as the additive character $\phi$ defining $\pi_{\infty}$ varies. In other words, these local systems are obtained from a single “master” $\widehat{G}$ -local system $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi)$ on the larger base $V^{*,\textup{st}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ by restriction to various $\mathbb{G}_{m}$ -orbits. Here the base space $V^{*,\textup{st}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is the stable locus of the dual vector space of $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ which is part of Vinberg’s $\theta$ -groups.

Second, when $\chi=1$ , the local system $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ has unipotent tame monodromy at $0$ given by a unipotent class $\underline{u}$ in $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ ( $\sigma$ is the pinned automorphism defining $G$ ), which only depends on the type of ${\mathbf{P}}$ . On the other hand, the types of ${\mathbf{P}}$ are in bijection with regular elliptic $\mathbb{W}$ -conjugacy classes $\underline{w}$ in $\mathbb{W}\sigma$ (at least when $\textup{char}(k)$ is large, see § 2.2 ). Therefore our construction gives a map $\{\mbox{regular elliptic $\mathbb{W}$-conjugacy classes in $\mathbb{W}\sigma$}\}\to\{\mbox{unipotent classes in $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$}\}$ sending $\underline{w}$ to $\underline{u}$ via the intermediate step of an admissible parahoric subgroup ${\mathbf{P}}$ . When $G$ is split, Theorem 4.7 gives an alternative description of this map using Lusztig’s theory of cells in affine Weyl groups, and using this we are able to compute the map ( 1.1 ) for all types of $G$ in § 4.8 (verified for split $G$ and conjectural in general). In [15] , Lusztig defined a map from all conjugacy classes in $\mathbb{W}$ to unipotent conjugacy classes in $\widehat{G}$ . One can check case-by-case that this map coincides with the restriction of Lusztig’s map to regular elliptic conjugacy classes.

As in the work of Frenkel and Gross [7] , we expect that there should be a parallel story when $\ell$ -adic local systems are replaced with connections on algebraic vector bundles (on varieties over $\mathbb{C}$ ). In particular, the “master” $\widehat{G}$ -local system $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi)$ should correspond to a $\widehat{G}$ -connection over $V^{*,\textup{st}}_{{\mathbf{P}},\mathbb{C}}$ . When $G$ is a classical group, the formulae in § 6 -§ 8 give descriptions of these connections as Fourier transform of Gauss-Manin connections. Are there simple formulae for these connection in general?

Another problem is to calculate the Euler characteristics (equivalently Swan conductor at $\infty$ ) of the local systems $\textup{Kl}^{V}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ for representations $V$ of $\widehat{G}$ . Such calculations would give evidence to (and sometimes proofs of) the prediction made by Reeder and Yu about the Langlands parameters of epipelagic representations (see § 2.5 ). We do one such calculation in the case $G$ is a unitary group (see Proposition 6.8 ), but in general the complexity of the calculation seems to be formidable (see also the proof of [10] in which we treated the case ${\mathbf{P}}$ is Iwahori, which already took many pages).

While we have a more or less complete picture for the local monodromy of the local systems $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ (partly conjectural), we do not discuss their global monodromy here, i.e., the Zariski closure of the image of the geometric $\pi_{1}(\mathbb{P}^{1}-\{0,\infty\})$ in $\widehat{G}$ . It can be as small as a finite group as we see in Proposition 6.7 (2) when $G$ is an odd unitary group and ${\mathbf{P}}$ is a special parahoric subgroup. Does the global monodromy group of $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ depend only on ${\mathbf{P}}$ , and if so, how do we read it off from ${\mathbf{P}}$ ?

Let $k$ be a finite field and $F=k(t)$ be the rational function field over $k$ . Places of $F$ are in natural bijection with closed points of $X:=\mathbb{P}^{1}_{k}$ , the set of which is denoted by $|X|$ . In particular we have two places $0$ and $\infty$ of $F$ . For a place $x\in|X|$ , let $F_{x}$ (resp. $\mathcal{O}_{x}$ ) be the completed local field (resp. completed local ring) of $X$ at $x$ , and let $k_{x}$ be the residue field at $x$ . Let $\mathbb{A}_{F}=\prod^{\prime}_{x\in|X|}F_{x}$ be the ring of adèles of $F$ .

Let $\ell$ be a prime number different from $\textup{char}(k)$ . We shall consider constructible $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}$ -complexes over various algebraic stacks over $k$ or $\bar{k}$ . All sheaf-theoretic operations are understood as derived functors .

Let $\mathbb{G}$ be a split reductive group over $k$ whose derived group is absolutely almost simple. Fix a pinning $\dagger=(\mathbb{B},\mathbb{T},\cdots)$ of $\mathbb{G}$ , where $\mathbb{B}$ is a Borel subgroup of $\mathbb{G}$ and $\mathbb{T}\subset\mathbb{B}$ a split maximal torus. Let $\mathbb{W}=N_{\mathbb{G}}(\mathbb{T})/\mathbb{T}$ be the Weyl group of $\mathbb{G}$ . Let $\Phi\subset\mathbb{X}^{*}(\mathbb{T})$ be the set of roots. Fix a cyclic subgroup $\mathbb{Z}/e\mathbb{Z}\hookrightarrow\textup{Aut}^{\dagger}(\mathbb{G})$ of the pinned automorphism group of $\mathbb{G}$ , and denote the image of $1$ by $\sigma\in\textup{Aut}^{\dagger}(\mathbb{G})$ .

We assume $\textup{char}(k)$ is prime to $e$ and that $k^{\times}$ contains $e$ th roots of unity.

Let $Z\mathbb{G}$ be the center of $\mathbb{G}$ . We also assume that $\mathbb{X}^{*}(Z\mathbb{G})^{\sigma}=0$ .

Let $\widetilde{X}\to X$ be the $\mu_{e}$ -cover totally ramified over $0$ and $\infty$ . Then $\widetilde{X}$ is also isomorphic to $\mathbb{P}^{1}_{k}$ , and we denote its affine coordinate by $t^{1/e}$ . We denote $X^{\circ}:=X-\{0,\infty\};\hskip 28.45274pt\widetilde{X}^{\circ}:=\widetilde{X}-\{0,\infty\}.$

The data $\mathbb{G}$ and $\sigma$ define a quasi-split group scheme $G$ over $X^{\circ}$ which splits over the $\mu_{e}$ -cover $\widetilde{X}^{\circ}\to X^{\circ}$ . More precisely, for any $k[t,t^{-1}]$ -algebra $R$ , we have $G(R)=\mathbb{G}(R\otimes_{k[t,t^{-1}]}k[t^{1/e},t^{-1/e}])^{\mu_{e}}$ where $\mu_{e}$ acts on $t^{1/e}$ by multiplication and on $\mathbb{G}$ via the fixed map $\mu_{e}\hookrightarrow\textup{Aut}^{\dagger}(\mathbb{G})$ . Since $\mathbb{X}^{*}(Z\mathbb{G})^{\sigma}=0$ , the center of $G$ does not contain a split torus.

Let $\SS$ be the neutral component of $\mathbb{T}^{\sigma}$ . Then $S=\SS\otimes_{k}F$ is a maximal split torus of $G$ over $F$ .

Let $\widehat{G}$ be the reductive group over $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}$ whose root system is dual to that of $\mathbb{G}$ . We also fix a pinned $\dagger$ of $\widehat{G}$ , through which we identify $\textup{Aut}^{\dagger}(\widehat{G})$ with $\textup{Aut}^{\dagger}(\mathbb{G})$ . We define the Langlands dual group ${\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}$ of $G$ to be ${\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}=\widehat{G}\rtimes\mu_{e}$ where $\mu_{e}$ acts through $\mu_{e}\hookrightarrow\textup{Aut}^{\dagger}(\mathbb{G})\cong\textup{Aut}^{\dagger}(\widehat{G})$ .

We shall use the convention in [21] for loop groups. For example, when $x\in X$ , we use $L_{x}G$ (resp. $L^{+}_{x}G$ , if $x\in X^{\circ}$ ) to denote the loop group (resp. positive loop group) of the group scheme $G$ at $x$ . Then $L_{x}G$ (resp. $L_{x}^{+}G$ ) is a group ind-scheme (resp. pro-algebraic group) over $k(x)$ , the residue field of $x$ . A parahoric subgroup of $G(F_{x})$ is also viewed as a pro-algebraic subgroup of $L_{x}G$ over $k(x)$ . Since $G$ splits over $\widetilde{X}^{\circ}$ , for $\widetilde{x}\in\widetilde{X}^{\circ}$ , we use $L_{\widetilde{x}}\mathbb{G}$ to denote the loop group of the constant group scheme $G\times_{X^{\circ}}\widetilde{X}^{\circ}\cong\mathbb{G}\times\widetilde{X}^{\circ}$ at $\widetilde{x}$ .

Since $G$ splits over $\widetilde{X}^{\circ}$ , for $\widetilde{x}\in\widetilde{X}^{\circ}$ , we use $L_{\widetilde{x}}\mathbb{G}$ to denote the loop group of the constant group scheme $G\times_{X^{\circ}}\widetilde{X}^{\circ}\cong\mathbb{G}\times\widetilde{X}^{\circ}$ at $\widetilde{x}$ .

2. Epipelagic representations and automorphic representations

In this section we let $K=F_{\infty}$ denote the local field of $F$ at $\infty$ . We first recall the construction of epipelagic representations of $G(K)$ following Reeder and Yu [18] . We then realize these epipelagic representations as the local components at $\infty$ of automorphic representations of $G(\mathbb{A}_{F})$ .

2.1. Admissible parahorics and $\theta$ -groups

The Borel subgroup $\mathbb{B}\subset\mathbb{G}$ allows us to define a standard Iwahori subgroup $\mathbf{I}\subset G(K)$ in the following way. Let $K_{e}$ be the totally ramified extension of $K$ of degree $e$ , then $G(K)\subset\mathbb{G}(K_{e})$ by construction. We define $\mathbf{I}$ to be the preimage of $\mathbb{B}$ under the homomorphism $G(K)\cap\mathbb{G}(\mathcal{O}_{K_{e}})\to\mathbb{G}(\mathcal{O}_{K_{e}})\twoheadrightarrow\mathbb{G}$ , the last map being given by $\mathcal{O}_{K_{e}}\twoheadrightarrow k$ .

Let ${\mathbf{P}}\subset G(K)$ be a standard parahoric subgroup (i.e., $\mathbf{I}\subset{\mathbf{P}}$ ). Let ${\mathbf{P}}\supset{\mathbf{P}}^{+}\supset{\mathbf{P}}^{++}$ be the first three steps in the Moy-Prasad filtration of ${\mathbf{P}}$ . In particular, ${\mathbf{P}}^{+}$ is the pro-unipotent radical of ${\mathbf{P}}$ and $L_{\mathbf{P}}:={\mathbf{P}}/{\mathbf{P}}^{+}$ is the Levi factor of ${\mathbf{P}}$ . We may view $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ as a subgroup of ${\mathbf{P}}$ containing $\SS=\mathbb{T}^{\sigma,\circ}$ . Let $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}={\mathbf{P}}^{+}/{\mathbf{P}}^{++}$ , a vector space over $k$ on which $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ acts. The pair $(L_{{\mathbf{P}}},V_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ is an example of a $\theta$ -group in the terminology of the Vinberg school.

The parahoric ${\mathbf{P}}$ is called admissible if there exists a closed orbit of $L_{\mathbf{P}}$ on the dual space $V^{*}_{\mathbf{P}}$ with finite stabilizers. Such orbits are called stable . Stable orbits form an open subset $V^{*,\textup{st}}_{\mathbf{P}}\subset V^{*}_{\mathbf{P}}$ . Elements in $V^{*,\textup{st}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ are called stable functionals on $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . For a complete list of admissible parahoric subgroups for various types of $G$ when $\textup{char}(k)$ is large, see the tables in [8] .

2.2. Relation with regular elliptic numbers

Let $\mathbb{W}^{\prime}=\mathbb{W}\rtimes\mu_{e}$ where $\mu_{e}$ acts on $\mathbb{W}$ via its pinned action on $\mathbb{G}$ . Springer [19] defined the notion of regular elements for $(\mathbb{W},\sigma)$ . We shall use a slightly different notion of $\mathbb{Z}$ -regularity defined by Gross, Levy, Reeder and Yu in [8] . An element $w\in\mathbb{W}^{\prime}$ is $\mathbb{Z}$ -regular if it permutes the roots $\Phi$ freely. See [8] for the relation between $\mathbb{Z}$ -regularity and Springer’s notion of regularity. Combining [8] and [19] one easily sees that a $\mathbb{Z}$ -regular element in $\mathbb{W}\sigma\subset\mathbb{W}^{\prime}$ is determined, up to $\mathbb{W}$ -conjugacy, by its order.

On the other hand, an element $w\in\mathbb{W}^{\prime}$ is elliptic if $\mathbb{X}^{*}(\mathbb{T}^{\textup{ad}})^{w}=0$ . The order of a $\mathbb{Z}$ -regular elliptic element in $\mathbb{W}\sigma\subset\mathbb{W}^{\prime}$ is called a regular elliptic number of the pair $(\mathbb{W},\sigma)$ . The above discussion says that the assignment $w\mapsto\textup{ord}(w)$ is a bijection between $\mathbb{Z}$ -regular elliptic elements in $\mathbb{W}\sigma$ up to $\mathbb{W}$ -conjugacy and regular elliptic number of the pair $(\mathbb{W},\sigma)$ .

To each standard parahoric ${\mathbf{P}}$ , we can assign a natural number $m$ as follows. Consider the apartment $\mathfrak{A}$ in the building of $G(K)$ corresponding to the maximal split torus $S=\SS\otimes_{k}F$ . Let $\xi$ be the barycenter of the facet corresponding to ${\mathbf{P}}$ . The number $m=m({\mathbf{P}})$ is defined as the smallest positive integer such that $\alpha(\xi)\in\frac{1}{m}\mathbb{Z}$ for all affine roots $\alpha$ of $G(K)$ with respect to $S$ (see [18] ).

When ${\mathbf{P}}$ is admissible, $\textup{char}(k)$ is not torsion for $G$ and $\textup{char}(k)\nmid m({\mathbf{P}})$ , it was shown in [18] that $m({\mathbf{P}})$ is a regular elliptic number of $(\mathbb{W},\sigma)$ , and conversely every regular elliptic number $m$ determines an admissible parahoric ${\mathbf{P}}$ up to conjugacy. Combing these facts, one sees that when $\textup{char}(k)$ is larger than the twisted Coxeter number $h_{\sigma}$ of $(\mathbb{G},\sigma)$ , there are natural bijections between the following three sets (1) Admissible standard parahorics ${\mathbf{P}}$ of $G(K)$ ; (2) $\mathbb{Z}$ -regular elliptic elements $w\in\mathbb{W}\sigma$ up to $\mathbb{W}$ -conjugacy; (3) Regular elliptic numbers $m$ of the pair $(\mathbb{W},\sigma)$ .

For a list of regular elliptic numbers in all types of $G$ , see § 4.8 .

(1) The Iwahori $\mathbf{I}$ is always admissible. It corresponds to the twisted Coxeter number $h_{\sigma}$ . In this case $L_{\mathbf{I}}=\mathbb{T}^{\sigma,\circ}$ , and $V_{\mathbf{I}}$ is the sum of affine simple root spaces of $G$ . (2) Let $\sigma\in\textup{Out}(\mathbb{G})$ by the opposition of the Dynkin diagram. Then $-1\in\mathbb{W}\sigma$ is a regular elliptic element, and $m=2$ is a regular elliptic number. The corresponding admissible ${\mathbf{P}}_{2}$ is a maximal parahoric except in type $A_{1}$ and $C_{n}$ . The Levi quotient $L_{2}\cong\mathbb{G}^{\theta,\circ}$ where $\theta$ is a Chevalley involution on $\mathbb{G}$ , and $V_{\mathbf{P}}$ can be identified with the $(-1)$ -eigenspace of $\theta$ on $\textup{Lie}\mathbb{G}$ . (3) Besides the case $m=2$ , there are a few more cases of admissible parahorics that are maximal: 
 
 ${\vphantom{D}}^{3}{D}_{4}\ (m=3),E_{6}\ (m=3),{\vphantom{E}}^{2}{E}_{6}\ (m=4),E_{8}\ (m=3,4,5)\textup{ and }F_{4}\ (m=3).$ (4) There are a few cases of admissible parahorics that are minimal (but not Iwahori). They are 
 
 ${\vphantom{A}}^{2}{A}_{2},{\vphantom{A}}^{2}{A}_{5},C_{2},D_{4},{\vphantom{D}}^{3}{D}_{4},E_{6},{\vphantom{E}}^{2}{E}_{6},E_{7},E_{8},F_{4},G_{2}.$ 
 In these cases, the admissible minimal parahoric corresponds to the unique vertex in the affine Dynkin diagram $\widetilde{\textup{Dyn}}(G)$ with three or more edges coming towards it.

From now on, till the end of § 5 , we shall fix an admissible standard parahoric subgroup ${\mathbf{P}}$ of $G(K)$ . Let $m=m({\mathbf{P}})$ as defined in § 2.2 .

2.4. Epipelagic supercuspidal representations

Fix a nontrivial character $\psi:k=\mathbb{F}_{q}\to\mathbb{Q}_{\ell}(\mu_{p})^{\times}$ . For a stable functional $\phi\in V^{*,\textup{st}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(k)$ , viewed as a linear map $\phi:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to k$ , Reeder-Yu [18] show that the compact induction 
 
 $\textup{c-Ind}^{G(K)}_{{\mathbf{P}}^{+}}(\psi\phi)$ 
 is a finite direct sum of irreducible supercuspidal representations of $G(K)$ (recall the center of $G(K)$ is finite). Its simple summands are called epipelagic representations of $G(K)$ attached to the parahoric ${\mathbf{P}}$ and the stable functional $\phi$ . These irreducible representations of $G(K)$ are characterized by having an $\psi\phi$ -eigenvector under ${\mathbf{P}}^{+}$ and having depth $1/m$ .

2.5. Expected Langlands parameter

Let $\mathcal{I}_{K}\lhd W(K^{s}/K)$ be the inertia group and the Weil group of $K$ . Let $\pi$ be an epipelagic representation of $G(K)$ attached to ${\mathbf{P}}$ and a stable linear functional $\phi:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to k$ . According the the local Langlands conjecture, there should be a Galois representation $\rho_{\pi}:W(K^{s}/K)\to{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell})$ attached to $\pi$ as the conjectural Langlands parameter. In particular, $\mathcal{I}_{K}$ acts on the Lie algebra ${\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}$ of $\widehat{G}$ by composing $\rho_{\pi}$ with the adjoint representation of $\widehat{G}$ . Guided by the conjecture in [9] relating the adjoint gamma factors and formal degrees, Reeder and Yu predicted in [18] that (1) ${\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{\mathcal{I}_{K}}=0$ ; (2) $\textup{Swan}({\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}})=\#\Phi/m$ . If moreover $\textup{char}(k)$ does not divide the order of $\mathbb{W}$ , Reeder and Yu made even sharper predictions: the restriction of $\rho_{\pi}$ to $\mathcal{I}_{K}$ should look like

 where such that a generator of the tame inertia $\mathcal{I}^{t}_{K}$ maps to a regular elliptic element $w\in\mathbb{W}\sigma$ of order $m$ .

2.6. Realization in automorphic representations

Recall $K=F_{\infty}$ and now we denote ${\mathbf{P}}$ by ${\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}$ . Let $\mathbf{I}_{0}\subset G(F_{0})$ be the Iwahori subgroup corresponding to the opposite Borel $\mathbb{B}^{\textup{opp}}$ of $\mathbb{G}$ , as $\mathbf{I}=\mathbf{I}_{\infty}$ was constructed from $\mathbb{B}$ . Let ${\mathbf{P}}_{0}\subset G(F_{0})$ be the parahoric subgroup containing $\mathbf{I}_{0}$ of the same type as ${\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}$ . Let $\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}$ be the normalizer of ${\mathbf{P}}_{0}$ in $G(F_{0})$ and $\Omega_{{\mathbf{P}}}=\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}/{\mathbf{P}}_{0}$ \footnote{ 1 One can show that $\Omega_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is always equal to the full stabilizer $\Omega$ of the fundamental alcove under the extended affine Weyl group of $G(K)$ . In other words, the sub-Dynkin diagram of the affine Dynkin diagram $\widetilde{\textup{Dyn}}(G)$ of $G$ representing an admissible standard parahoric ${\mathbf{P}}$ is always stable under the automorphisms of $\widetilde{\textup{Dyn}}(G)$ fixing the extended node.} . The Levi quotient of ${\mathbf{P}}_{0}$ is again identified with $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Let $L^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}=L_{{\mathbf{P}}}/L_{{\mathbf{P}}}^{\textup{der}}$ be the maximal quotient torus of $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Let ${\mathbf{P}}^{\textup{ss}}_{0}=\ker({\mathbf{P}}_{0}\to L^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ and let 
 
 $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}=\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}/{\mathbf{P}}^{\textup{ss}}_{0}.$ 
 Note that $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is an extension of the finite group $\Omega_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ by the torus $L^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ .

Fix a character $\chi:\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{\mathbf{P}}(k)\to\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}^{\times}$ and a stable linear functional $\phi:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to k$ .

Now we try to classify automorphic representations $\pi=\otimes^{\prime}_{x\in|X|}\pi_{x}$ of $G(\mathbb{A}_{F})$ satisfying • $\pi_{x}$ is unramified for $x\neq 0,\infty$ ; • $\pi_{0}$ has an eigenvector under $\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}$ on which it acts through $\chi$ via the quotient $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ ; • $\pi_{\infty}$ has an eigenvector under ${\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}$ on which it acts through $\psi\phi$ (i.e., $\pi_{\infty}$ is an epipelagic supercuspidal representation attached to ${\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}$ and the stable functional $\phi$ ).

There is a unique automorphic representation $\pi=\pi(\chi,\phi)$ of $G(\mathbb{A}_{F})$ satisfying all the above conditions. Moreover, we have (1) $\pi$ is cuspidal, and appears with multiplicity one in the automorphic spectrum of $G$ . (2) Both eigenspaces $\pi_{0}^{(\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0},\chi)}$ and $\pi_{\infty}^{({\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}^{+},\psi\phi)}$ are one-dimensional.

Consider the vector space of $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}$ -valued functions 
 
 $\mathcal{A}:=\textup{Fun}\left(G(F)\backslash G(\mathbb{A}_{F})/\prod_{x\neq 0,\infty}G(\mathcal{O}_{x})\right)^{(\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0},\chi)\times({\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty},\psi\phi)}.$ 
 Here the superscript means taking the eigenspace under $\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}\times{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}$ on which $\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}$ acts through $\chi$ and ${\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}$ acts through $\psi\phi$ . We first show that $\dim\mathcal{A}=1$ .

Let $\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}=G(k[t,t^{-1}])\cap\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}$ . Then $\widetilde{L}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is also a subgroup of $\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}$ . Let $W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ be the Weyl group of $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ , identified with a subgroup of the extended affine Weyl group $\widetilde{W}$ of $G(F_{\infty})$ (both are with respect to $\SS$ ). Let $\widetilde{W}_{{\mathbf{P}}}=N_{\widetilde{W}}(W_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ , which is an extension of $\Omega_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ by $W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ .

A parahoric variant of [10] gives an equality between double cosets 
 
 $G(F)\backslash G(\mathbb{A}_{F})/(\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}\times\prod_{x\neq 0,\infty}G(\mathcal{O}_{x})\times{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty})=\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\backslash G(F_{\infty})/{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}$ 
 and the Birkhoff decomposition 
 
 $G(F_{\infty})=\bigsqcup_{\widetilde{w}\in\widetilde{W}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\backslash\widetilde{W}/W_{{\mathbf{P}}}}\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\widetilde{w}{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}.$ 


Recall the apartment $\mathfrak{A}$ and the barycenter $\xi$ of the facet corresponding to ${\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}$ as in § 2.2 . The set $\Psi_{\textup{aff}}$ of affine roots of $G(F_{\infty})$ are certain affine functions on $\mathfrak{A}$ . For a subgroup $\mathbf{K}$ of $G(F_{\infty})$ , we use $\Psi(\mathbf{K})$ to denote the set of affine roots whose root subgroups are contained in $\mathbf{K}$ . Therefore $\Psi({\mathbf{P}}_{\infty})=\{\alpha\in\Psi_{\textup{aff}}|\alpha(\xi)\geq 0\}$ , $\Psi(\Gamma_{0})=\{\alpha\in\Psi_{\textup{aff}}|\alpha(\xi)\leq 0\}$ , and $\Psi(L_{{\mathbf{P}}})=\{\alpha\in\Psi_{\textup{aff}}|\alpha(\xi)=0\}$

By the definition of $m=m({\mathbf{P}})$ , we have $\alpha(\xi)\in\frac{1}{m}\mathbb{Z}$ . Moreover, since $\xi$ is the barycenter of its facet, $\Psi({\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty})-\Psi({\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty})=\{\alpha\in\Psi_{\textup{aff}}|\alpha(\xi)=\frac{1}{m}\}$ (these are exactly the affine roots that appear in $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ ), and $\Psi({\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty})=\{\alpha\in\Psi_{\textup{aff}}|\alpha(\xi)\geq\frac{2}{m}\}$ .

Suppose $f$ is nonzero on the double coset $\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\widetilde{w}\ell{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}$ for some $\widetilde{w}\in\widetilde{W}$ and $\ell\in L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Note that $f$ is a function on the quotient $\Gamma^{\textup{ss}}_{0}\backslash(\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\widetilde{w}\ell{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty})/{\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty}$ (where $\Gamma^{\textup{ss}}_{0}:=\ker(\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\to\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ ), and is $(V_{{\mathbf{P}}},\psi\phi)$ -eigen. For $\alpha\in\Psi({\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty})-\Psi({\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty})$ (equivalently, $\alpha(\xi)=\frac{1}{m}$ ), we identify the affine root subgroup of $\alpha$ with the $\alpha$ -weight space $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\alpha)$ of $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Suppose also $\widetilde{w}\alpha\in\Psi(\Gamma^{\textup{ss}}_{0})$ (equivalently $(\widetilde{w}\alpha)(\xi)\leq 0$ ). then for any $u_{\alpha}\in V_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\alpha)$ , we have 
 
 $\psi(\langle{\phi,\textup{Ad}(\ell^{-1})u_{\alpha}}\rangle)f(\widetilde{w}\ell)=f(\widetilde{w}\ell\textup{Ad}(\ell^{-1})u_{\alpha})=f(\widetilde{w}u_{\alpha}\ell)=f(\textup{Ad}(\widetilde{w})u_{\alpha}\widetilde{w}\ell)=f(\widetilde{w}\ell).$ 
 Therefore we must have $\langle{\phi,\textup{Ad}(\ell^{-1})V_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\alpha)}\rangle=0$ . The annihilator of $\bigoplus_{\alpha(\xi)=\frac{1}{m},(\widetilde{w}\alpha)(\xi)\leq 0}V_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\alpha)\subset V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ in the dual space is $V^{*,\widetilde{w}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}:=\bigoplus_{\alpha(\xi)=\frac{1}{m},(\widetilde{w}\alpha)(\xi)>0}V_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\alpha)^{*}=\bigoplus_{\beta(\xi)=-\frac{1}{m},(\widetilde{w}\beta)(\xi)<0}V^{*}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\alpha)$ . Then the above discussion shows that the orbit $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}\cdot\phi$ intersects $V^{*,\widetilde{w}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ .

For $\beta(\xi)=\frac{1}{m}$ , the condition $(\widetilde{w}\beta)(\xi)<0$ is equivalent to saying that $(\widetilde{w}\alpha)(\xi)\leq\alpha(\xi)$ , or $\langle{\bar{\beta},\widetilde{w}^{-1}\xi-\xi}\rangle\leq 0$ , where $\bar{\beta}$ is the vector part of $\beta$ . In other words, in the weight decomposition of $V^{*,\widetilde{w}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ under the torus $\SS$ , a weight $\bar{\beta}\in\mathbb{X}^{*}(\SS)$ appears if and only if it satisfies $\langle{\bar{\beta},\widetilde{w}^{-1}\xi-\xi}\rangle\leq 0$ .

Suppose $\widetilde{w}^{-1}\xi\neq\xi$ , let $\lambda=m(\widetilde{w}^{-1}\xi-\xi)\in\mathbb{X}_{*}(\SS)$ . Any point of $V^{*,\widetilde{w}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}=\oplus_{\langle{\bar{\beta},\lambda}\rangle\leq 0}V^{*}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\beta)$ under the action of the one-dimensional torus $\lambda(\mathbb{G}_{m})$ has a limit point in $\oplus_{\langle{\bar{\beta},\lambda}\rangle=0}V^{*}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\beta)$ . Since the orbit $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}\cdot\phi$ is closed, it contains a point $\phi^{\prime}\in\oplus_{\langle{\bar{\beta},\lambda}\rangle=0}V^{*}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(\beta)$ . But then the torus $\lambda(\mathbb{G}_{m})$ fixes $\phi^{\prime}$ , contradicting the assumption that $\phi$ , hence $\phi^{\prime}$ , should have finite stabilizer under $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . This shows that $f$ must be zero on $\Gamma\widetilde{w}{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}$ for $\widetilde{w}^{-1}\xi\neq\xi$ .

Those $\widetilde{w}$ with $\widetilde{w}^{-1}\xi=\xi$ are precisely those in $\widetilde{W}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ , therefore, $f$ , as a function on $G(F_{\infty})$ , is supported on the unit coset $\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}=\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\times{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}$ . The $(\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0},\chi)$ -eigen property of $f$ as a function on $G(\mathbb{A}_{F})$ implies that it is left $(\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0},\chi)$ -eigen as a function on $G(F_{\infty})$ . Together with the right $({\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty},\psi\phi)$ -eigen property, the function $f$ is unique up to a scalar. We have shown that $\dim\mathcal{A}=1$ .

Next we check that $\mathcal{A}$ consists of cuspidal functions. In fact, for any $f\in\mathcal{A}$ , $g\in G(\mathbb{A}_{F})$ and any proper $F$ -parabolic $P\subset G$ with unipotent radical $U_{P}$ , the constant term $\int_{U_{P}(F)\backslash U_{P}(\mathbb{A}_{F})}f(ng)dn$ can be written as a finite sum of the form $h(g_{\infty}):=\int_{\Gamma\backslash U_{P}(F_{\infty})}f(g^{\infty},n_{\infty}g_{\infty})dn_{\infty}$ , for some discrete subgroup $\Gamma\subset U_{P}(F_{\infty})$ and $g=(g^{\infty},g_{\infty})$ where $g_{\infty}\in G(F_{\infty}),g^{\infty}\in G(\mathbb{A}^{\infty}_{F})$ . The function $h:G(F_{\infty})\to\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}$ defined above is left $U_{P}(F_{\infty})$ -invariant and right $({\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty},\psi\phi)$ -eigen, hence induces a $U_{P}(F_{\infty})$ -invariant map $c_{h}:\textup{c-Ind}^{G(F_{\infty})}_{{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}}(\bar{\psi}\phi)\to\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}$ by $h^{\prime}\mapsto\int_{G(F_{\infty})/{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}}h(x)h^{\prime}(x^{-1})dx$ . However, as we discussed in § 2.4 , $\textup{c-Ind}^{G(F_{\infty})}_{{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}}(\bar{\psi}\phi)$ is a finite direct sum of supercuspidal representations, hence has zero Jacquet module with respect to $P$ . Therefore $c_{h}=0$ and $h$ must be the zero function. This implies that the constant term $\int_{U_{P}(F)\backslash U_{P}(\mathbb{A}_{F})}f(ng)dn$ must be zero.

Since cuspidal functions belong to the discrete spectrum, we have 
 
 (2.1)  $\dim\mathcal{A}=\sum_{\pi}m(\pi)\dim\pi_{0}^{(\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0},\chi)}\dim\pi_{\infty}^{({\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}^{+},\psi\phi)}.$ 
 where the sum is over isomorphism classes of $\pi$ satisfying the conditions in § 2.6 , and $m(\pi)$ is the multiplicity that $\pi$ appears in the automorphic spectrum of $G(\mathbb{A}_{F})$ . Since $\dim\mathcal{A}=1$ , the right side of ( 2.1 ) only has one nonzero term, in which all factors are equal to one. ∎

3. Generalized Kloosterman sheaves

We keep the notations from § 2.6 . In this section, we will construct generalized Kloosterman sheaves as Galois representations attached to the automorphic representations $\pi(\chi,\phi)$ in Proposition 2.7 . The construction uses ideas from the geometric Langlands correspondence.

3.1. A sheaf on the moduli space of $G$ -bundles

Let $\textup{Bun}_{G}({\mathbf{P}}^{\textup{ss}}_{0},{\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty})$ be the moduli stack of $G$ -bundles over $X=\mathbb{P}^{1}$ with ${\mathbf{P}}^{\textup{ss}}_{0}$ -level structures at $0$ and ${\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty}$ -level structures at $\infty$ . This is an algebraic stack over $k$ . For the construction of moduli stacks with parahoric level structures, such as $\textup{Bun}_{G}({\mathbf{P}}_{0},{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty})$ , see [20] ; the moduli stack $\textup{Bun}_{G}({\mathbf{P}}^{\textup{ss}}_{0},{\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty})$ is an $L^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times({\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}/{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}^{++})$ -torsor over $\textup{Bun}_{G}({\mathbf{P}}_{0},{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty})$ . In the sequel we abbreviate $\textup{Bun}:=\textup{Bun}_{G}({\mathbf{P}}^{\textup{ss}}_{0},{\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty})$ . The trivial $G$ -bundle with the tautological level structures at $0$ and $\infty$ gives a base point $\star\in\textup{Bun}(k)$ .

There is an action of $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ on Bun because $\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0}$ normalizes ${\mathbf{P}}^{\textup{ss}}_{0}$ and ${\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}^{+}$ normalizes ${\mathbf{P}}^{++}_{\infty}$ . The character $\chi$ defines a rank one Kummer local system $\mathcal{L}_{\chi}$ on $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ such that its Frobenius traces at $k$ -rational points give back the character $\chi:\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(k)\to\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}^{\times}$ . Let $S$ be a scheme and $s:S\to V^{*}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ be a morphism. We also consider $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S$ as a constant additive group over $S$ , over which we have a rank one local system $\textup{AS}_{S}:=(\textup{id}\times s)^{*}\langle{,}\rangle^{*}\textup{AS}_{\psi}$ where $\langle{,}\rangle:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times V^{*}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to\mathbb{G}_{a}$ is the natural pairing and $\textup{AS}_{\psi}$ is the rank one Artin-Schreier local system on $\mathbb{G}_{a}$ given by the additive character $\psi$ .

Both $\mathcal{L}_{\chi}$ and $\textup{AS}_{S}$ are character sheaves. We spell out what this means for $\textup{AS}_{S}$ . Let $a:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S\to V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S$ be the addition map in the first two variables, and let $p_{23}:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S\to V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S$ be the projection onto the last two factors. Then there is an isomorphism $a^{*}\textup{AS}_{S}\cong p_{23}^{*}\textup{AS}_{S}$ , which satisfies the usual cocycle relation when pulled back to $V^{3}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S$ .

For any scheme $S$ over $V^{*}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ , we consider the product $\textup{Bun}\times S$ , on which the algebraic group $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ and the constant group scheme $V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S$ acts. We may then talk about the derived category of $(\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}},\mathcal{L}_{\chi})$ -equivariant and $(V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times S,\textup{AS}_{S})$ -equivariant $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}$ -complex on $\textup{Bun}\times S$ . We denote this category by $\mathcal{D}(\chi,S)$ .

Let $\textup{Bun}_{G}(\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0},{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty})$ be the quotient of Bun by the $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ -action. The parahoric analog of the Birkhoff decomposition [10] gives a decomposition 
 
 (3.1)  $\textup{Bun}_{G}(\widetilde{{\mathbf{P}}}_{0},{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty})(k)\cong\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\backslash G(F_{\infty})/{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}=\bigsqcup_{w\in\widetilde{W}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\backslash\widetilde{W}/W_{{\mathbf{P}}}}\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\backslash(\widetilde{\Gamma}_{0}\widetilde{w}{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty})/{\mathbf{P}}^{+}_{\infty}.$ 
 Moreover, the same is true when $k$ is replaced by its algebraic closure (and with $G(F_{\infty}),{\mathbf{P}}_{\infty}$ etc. base changed to $\bar{k}$ ). By the parahoric analog of [10] , when $\widetilde{w}=1$ , the unit double coset is an open point with trivial stabilizer, which is also the image of $\star$ . The preimage $U$ of this open point in Bun is then a $\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times V_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ -torsor trivialized by the base point $\star$ , giving an open immersion 
 
 $j:U\cong\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\times V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\hookrightarrow\textup{Bun}.$

4. Unipotent monodromy

The goal of this section is to study the monodromy of the Kloosterman sheaves $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ at $0$ , especially when $\chi=1$ .

4.1. Lusztig’s theory of (two-sided) cells

Let $W_{\textup{aff}}$ be the affine Weyl group attached to $G(K)$ . There is a partition of $W_{\textup{aff}}$ into finitely many two-sided cells . There is a partial order among these cells. The largest cell is the singleton $\{1\}$ ; the smallest one contains the longest element $w_{0}$ in the Weyl group $W_{\mathbf{Q}}$ of a special parahoric subgroup $\mathbf{Q}$ . Each cell $\underline{c}$ is assigned an integer $a(\underline{c})$ satisfying $0\leq a(\underline{c})\leq\min\{\ell(w)|w\in\underline{c}\}$ .

Let $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ be the neutral component of the fixed point of $\sigma$ on $\widehat{G}$ .

There is an order preserving bijection 
 
 $\{\textup{two-sided cells in }W_{\textup{aff}}\}\longleftrightarrow\{\textup{unipotent conjugacy classes in }\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}\}$ 
 such that if $\underline{c}\leftrightarrow\underline{u}$ , then $a(\underline{c})=\dim\mathcal{B}_{u}$ where $\mathcal{B}_{u}$ is the Springer fiber of $u\in\underline{u}$ (inside the flag variety of $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ ).

Lusztig’s paper only dealt with the case of split $G$ . However, for our quasi-split $G$ , the affine Weyl group $W_{\textup{aff}}$ is isomorphic to the affine Weyl group of a split group $G^{\prime}$ as Coxeter groups. Moreover, one can choose $G^{\prime}$ so that $\widehat{G}^{\prime}=\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ . Therefore the quasi-split case of the above theorem follows from the split case.

For a standard parahoric subgroup ${\mathbf{P}}\subset G(K)$ , let $\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ be the two-sided cell in $W_{\textup{aff}}$ containing the longest element $w_{{\mathbf{P}}}\in W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ (the Weyl group of $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ ). We define $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ to be the unipotent class of $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ corresponding to $\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ under Theorem 4.2 .

For any standard parahoric ${\mathbf{P}}$ , we have $a(\underline{c}_{\mathbf{P}})=\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ .

We may assume $G$ is simply-connected. Let $a=\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ . Let $\mathbf{H}:=C_{c}(\mathbf{I}\backslash G(K)/\mathbf{I})$ be the Iwahori Hecke algebra and let $\{C_{w}\}_{w\in W_{\textup{aff}}}$ be its Kazhdan-Lusztig basis. By definition, $a(\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ is equal to $a(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ , which is the largest power of $q^{1/2}$ appearing in the coefficient of $C_{w_{{\mathbf{P}}}}$ in $C_{x}C_{y}$ for some $x,y\in W_{\textup{aff}}$ . Since $C_{w_{{\mathbf{P}}}}^{2}=f(q^{1/2})C_{w_{{\mathbf{P}}}}$ for $f(q^{1/2})=q^{-a/2}\sum_{x\in W_{{\mathbf{P}}}}q^{\ell(x)}$ whose highest degree term is $q^{a/2}$ , we have $a(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})\geq a$ . On the other hand, one always has $a(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})\leq\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})=a$ by [15] , therefore $a(\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}})=a(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})=a$ . ∎

We now give an alternative description of the unipotent class $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . To state it, we recall the truncated induction defined by Lusztig [13] in a special case, which was first constructed by Macdonald [17] . Let $W=\mathbb{W}^{\sigma}$ be the $F$ -Weyl group of $G$ (this is also $W_{\mathbf{Q}}$ for a special parahoric $\mathbf{Q}$ , and is also the Weyl group of $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ ), then $\mathfrak{a}=\mathbb{X}_{*}(\mathbb{T})^{\sigma}_{\mathbb{C}}$ is the reflection representation of $W$ . The sign representation $\epsilon$ of $W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ appears in $\textup{Res}^{W}_{W_{{\mathbf{P}}}}\textup{Sym}^{\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})}(\mathfrak{a})$ with multiplicity one. The truncated induction $j^{W}_{W_{{\mathbf{P}}}}(\epsilon)$ is the unique irreducible $W$ -submodule of $\textup{Sym}^{\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})}(\mathfrak{a})$ that contains the sign representation of $W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ .

Let ${\mathbf{P}}$ be a standard parahoric subgroup of $G(K)$ . (1) Under the Springer correspondence, $j^{W}_{W_{{\mathbf{P}}}}(\epsilon)$ corresponds to the unipotent class $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ of $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ together with the trivial local system on it. (2) Suppose ${\mathbf{P}}\subset\mathbf{Q}$ for some special parahoric $\mathbf{Q}$ , then ${\mathbf{P}}$ corresponds to a standard parabolic subgroup $P\subset L_{\mathbf{Q}}$ , hence also to a standard parabolic subgroup $\widehat{P}\subset\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ . Then $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is the Richardson class attached to $\widehat{P}$ (i.e., the unipotent class in $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ that contains a dense open subset of the unipotent radical of $\widehat{P}$ ).

Remark 4.3 allows us to reduce to the case $G$ is split.

(1) Let $\underline{u}$ (together with the trivial local system on it) be the unipotent class in $\widehat{G}$ that corresponds to $j_{W_{{\mathbf{P}}}}^{W}(\epsilon)$ under the Springer correspondence. Springer theory tells that $\dim\mathcal{B}_{u}=\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ for any $u\in\underline{u}$ .

Recall $\mathbf{H}:=C_{c}(\mathbf{I}\backslash G(K)/\mathbf{I})$ is the Iwahori Hecke algebra and let $\mathbf{H}_{{\mathbf{P}}}:=C(\mathbf{I}\backslash{\mathbf{P}}/\mathbf{I})$ be its parahoric subalgebra. Let $M$ be an irreducible $\mathbf{H}$ -module such that $M^{\mathbf{H}_{{\mathbf{P}}}}\neq 0$ and that $C_{w}$ acts on $M$ by zero for any $w$ lying in cells $\underline{c}\leq\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Such an $\mathbf{H}$ -module can be found in the cell subquotient $\mathbf{H}_{\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}}}$ . The Langlands parameter of $M$ is a triple $(u^{\prime},s,\rho)$ where $u^{\prime}$ is a unipotent class in $\widehat{G}$ . By the construction of Lusztig’s bijection Theorem 4.2 , $u^{\prime}\in\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Consider the $\mathbf{H}$ -module ${\vphantom{M}}^{*}{M}$ (changing the action of $T_{s}$ to $-qT^{-1}_{s}$ for simple reflections $s\in W_{\textup{aff}}$ , see [14] ), then ${\vphantom{M}}^{*}{M}$ contains a copy of the sign representation of $\mathbf{H}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Applying [14] to ${\vphantom{M}}^{*}{M}$ , we conclude that $u^{\prime}$ lies in the closure of $\underline{u}$ . Since we also have $\dim\mathcal{B}_{u^{\prime}}=a(\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}})=\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ by Theorem 4.2 and Lemma 4.5 , we must have $\underline{u}=\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ .

(2) follows from [12] . ∎

The main result of this section is

Assume $\chi=1$ and $G$ is split. Then for any stable functional $\phi:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to k$ , the local monodromy of the $\widehat{G}$ -local system $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ at $0$ is tame and is given by the conjugacy class $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ .

The proof of the theorem relies on deep results of Lusztig and Bezrukavnikov on cells in affine Weyl groups, and occupies § 4.10 to § 4.16 .

If ${\mathbf{P}}=\mathbf{I}$ , then $\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is the maximal cell, hence $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is the regular unipotent class. This case of the theorem was proved in [10] . Another extremal case is when ${\mathbf{P}}$ is a special parahoric of $G(K)$ (this happens only when $G$ is an odd unitary group and $m=2$ ), the quasi-split analog of the above theorem should say that $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ is unramified at $0$ , which is the case as we will see in Proposition 6.7 (2).

4.9. Conjectural description of the tame monodromy for general $\chi$

Finally we give a conjectural description of the local monodromy of $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ at $0$ for general $\chi:\widetilde{L}^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}^{\times}$ and quasi-split $G$ . First of all $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ is tame at $0$ . Recall the tame inertia $\mathcal{I}^{t}_{0}\cong\varprojlim_{(n,p)=1}\mu_{n}(\bar{k})$ and in particular $\mathcal{I}^{t}_{0}\twoheadrightarrow\mu_{e}(k)$ . Let $\xi\in\mathcal{I}^{t}_{0}$ be a generator that maps to $\sigma\in\mu_{e}$ . We predict that under the monodromy representation $\mathcal{I}^{t}_{0}\to{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}$ associated with $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\chi,\phi)$ , $\xi$ should go to $(\kappa u,\sigma)\in\widehat{G}^{\sigma}\times{\sigma}\subset{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G}$ where $\kappa u$ is the Jordan decomposition into the semisimple part and unipotent part of an element in $\widehat{G}^{\sigma}$ .

We first describe the semisimple part $\kappa$ . Up to $\widehat{G}$ -conjugacy we may arrange that $\kappa\in\widehat{T}$ . By further $\widehat{T}$ -conjugation, only the image of $\kappa$ in the $\sigma$ -coinvariants $\widehat{T}_{\sigma}$ is well-defined. The character $k^{\times}\otimes\mathbb{X}_{*}(\mathbb{T})^{\sigma}=\SS(k)\to L^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}\xrightarrow{\chi}\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}^{\times}$ gives a homomorphism $\chi^{\prime}:k^{\times}\to\widehat{T}_{\sigma}=\textup{Hom}(\mathbb{X}_{*}(\mathbb{T})^{\sigma},\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}^{\times})$ . Local class field theory identifies $k^{\times}$ with the tame inertia of $\textup{Gal}(F^{\textup{ab}}_{0}/F_{0})$ (where $F^{\textup{ab}}_{0}$ is a maximal abelian extension of $F_{0}$ ), hence there is a canonical surjection $\mathcal{I}^{t}_{0}\twoheadrightarrow k^{\times}$ . Composing with $\chi^{\prime}$ we get a homomorphism $\chi^{\prime\prime}:\mathcal{I}^{t}_{0}\to\widehat{T}_{\sigma}$ . The image of $\kappa$ in $\widehat{T}_{\sigma}$ should be $\chi^{\prime\prime}(\xi)$ . This should be provable using similar techniques as in the proof of [21] , based on results in [1] .

Next we describe the unipotent part $u$ . Let $W_{\kappa,\textup{aff}}\subset W_{\textup{aff}}$ be the subgroup generated by affine reflections whose vector part $\alpha\in\Phi$ is such that the composition $\mathbb{G}_{m}(k)\xrightarrow{\alpha^{\vee}}\SS(k)\to L^{\textup{ab}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}(k)\xrightarrow{\chi}\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}^{\times}$ is trivial. It can be shown that $W_{\kappa,\textup{aff}}$ is an affine Coxeter group. In fact $W_{\kappa,\textup{aff}}$ can be identified with the affine Weyl group of the group dual to $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}_{\kappa}$ , the centralizer in $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}$ of an element $\widetilde{\kappa}\in\widehat{T}^{\sigma,\circ}$ lifting $\kappa\in\widehat{T}_{\sigma}$ . The longest element $w_{{\mathbf{P}}}\in W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ belongs to $W_{\kappa,\textup{aff}}$ , hence lies in a two-sided cell $\underline{c}_{\kappa,{\mathbf{P}}}$ of $W_{\kappa,\textup{aff}}$ . By Lusztig’s Theorem 4.2 , $\underline{c}_{\kappa,{\mathbf{P}}}$ corresponds to a unipotent class $\underline{u}_{\kappa,{\mathbf{P}}}$ of $\widehat{G}^{\sigma,\circ}_{\kappa}$ . The unipotent element $u$ should lie in $\underline{u}_{\kappa,{\mathbf{P}}}$ . This should be provable, in the same way as Theorem 4.7 , using the results of Bezrukavnikov et al. in [2] , which are monodromic analogue of his results in [1] .

4.10. Hecke operators at $0$

The rest of this section is devoted to the proof of Theorem 4.7 . We first set up some notation. Fix a stable functional $\phi:V_{{\mathbf{P}}}\to k$ . Since $\chi=1$ , we simply denote the Kloosterman sheaf $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ by $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ . Comparing a general split $G$ with its simply-connected cover $G^{\textup{sc}}$ , it is easy to see that the local system $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ induces the local system $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G^{\textup{sc}}},m}(\phi)$ via the map $\widehat{G}\to\widehat{G^{\textup{sc}}}=\widehat{G}^{\textup{ad}}$ . Therefore it suffices to prove Theorem 4.7 for simply-connected $G$ .

In the sequel we shall base change all spaces to $\bar{k}$ without changing notation. When $S$ is a point $\phi:\textup{Spec}\ \bar{k}\to V^{*,\textup{st}}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ , we denote the category of automorphic sheaves $\mathcal{D}(1,S)$ by $\mathcal{D}(\phi)$ . 4.13. Serre quotients of $\mathcal{P}$

The category $\mathcal{P}=\textup{Perv}({\mathbf{P}}_{0}\backslash L_{0}G/{\mathbf{P}}_{0})$ is not closed under the convolution $\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}$ in general. There is a universal way to fix this problem. Let $\mathcal{N}\subset\mathcal{P}$ be the Serre subcategory of $\mathcal{P}$ generated (under extensions) by irreducible objects that appear as simple constituents of ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})$ for some $C_{1},C_{2}\in\mathcal{P}$ and $i\neq 0$ . Consider the Serre quotient $\overline{\mathcal{P}}:=\mathcal{P}/\mathcal{N}$ .

The functor ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{0}(-\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}-):\mathcal{P}\times\mathcal{P}\to\mathcal{P}\twoheadrightarrow\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ factors through $\overline{\mathcal{P}}\times\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ and and it defines a monoidal structure $\circledast:\overline{\mathcal{P}}\times\overline{\mathcal{P}}\to\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ on $\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ .

We show that if an irreducible object $C\in\mathcal{N}$ , then ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{0}(C\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C^{\prime})\in\mathcal{N}$ for any $C^{\prime}\in\mathcal{P}$ . By definition $C$ is a subquotient of ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})$ for some $i\neq 0$ and $C_{1},C_{2}$ simple objects in $\mathcal{P}$ . By the decomposition theorem, $C$ is in fact a direct summand of ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})$ . Therefore it suffices to show that ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{0}({\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C^{\prime})\in\mathcal{N}$ . Again by the decomposition theorem, the spectral sequence ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{j}({\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C^{\prime})\Rightarrow{\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i+j}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C^{\prime})$ degenerates at $E_{2}$ , therefore ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{0}({\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C^{\prime})$ is a subquotient of ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C^{\prime})$ , hence in $\mathcal{N}$ since $i\neq 0$ . This shows that the functor ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{0}(-\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}-)$ factors through $\overline{\mathcal{P}}\times\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ . The associativity of $\circledast$ can be proved in the same way. ∎

Let $a$ be the length of the longest element of $W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ , so we have $a=a(\underline{c})$ by Lemma 4.5 . Let $\pi:\textup{Fl}=L_{0}G/\mathbf{I}_{0}\to\textup{Fl}_{{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}=L_{0}G/{\mathbf{P}}_{0}$ be the projection. Let $\mathcal{Q}=\textup{Perv}(\mathbf{I}_{0}\backslash L_{0}G/\mathbf{I}_{0})$ . Since $\pi$ is smooth of relative dimension $a$ , the functor $\pi^{\natural}:=\pi^{*}[a](a/2)$ gives an exact functor $\mathcal{P}\to\mathcal{Q}$ that is also fully faithful (we fix a square root of $q=\#k$ , hence the half Tate twist makes sense). We may therefore identify $\mathcal{P}$ with a full subcategory of $\mathcal{Q}$ via the functor $\pi^{\natural}$ .

The category $\mathcal{Q}$ carries a filtration by full subcategories indexed by two-sided cells in $W_{\textup{aff}}$ . Let $\underline{c}=\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ be the cell containing the longest element in $W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ . Then $\mathcal{P}\subset\mathcal{Q}_{\leq\underline{c}}$ . The category $\mathcal{P}_{<\underline{c}}:=\mathcal{P}\cap\mathcal{Q}_{<\underline{c}}$ is a Serre subcategory (generated by those irreducible objects in $\mathcal{P}$ indexed by longest representatives of $(W_{{\mathbf{P}}},W_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ -double cosets in $W_{\textup{aff}}$ that belong to a cell smaller than $\underline{c}$ ). Let $\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}:=\mathcal{P}/\mathcal{P}_{<\underline{c}}$ be the Serre quotient. This is a full subcategory of a similar Serre quotient $\mathcal{Q}_{\underline{c}}:=\mathcal{Q}_{\leq\underline{c}}/\mathcal{Q}_{<\underline{c}}$ . It is known that $\mathcal{Q}_{\underline{c}}$ carries a monoidal structure $\odot$ given by truncated convolution: $\widetilde{C}_{1}\odot\widetilde{C}_{2}$ is the image of ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{a}(\widetilde{C}_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle\mathbf{I}_{0}}}{{*}}\widetilde{C}_{2})(a/2)$ in $\mathcal{Q}_{\underline{c}}$ .

The category $\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}\subset\mathcal{Q}_{\underline{c}}$ is closed under the truncated convolution $\odot$ . The quotient functor $\mathcal{P}\twoheadrightarrow\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ factors through $\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ and induces a monoidal Serre quotient functor 
 
 $\eta:(\overline{\mathcal{P}},\circledast)\twoheadrightarrow(\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}},\odot)$ 


For $C_{1},C_{2}\in\mathcal{P}$ with image $\widetilde{C}_{1}$ and $\widetilde{C}_{2}$ in $\mathcal{Q}$ , we have 
 
 (4.3)  $\pi^{\natural}C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle\mathbf{I}_{0}}}{{*}}\pi^{\natural}C_{2}\cong\pi^{\natural}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})\otimes\textup{H}^{*}({{\mathbf{P}}_{0}/\mathbf{I}_{0}})[a](a/2).$ 


We first show that $\mathcal{N}\subset\mathcal{P}_{<\underline{c}}$ so the quotient functor factors as $\overline{\mathcal{P}}\twoheadrightarrow\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ . For a simple perverse sheaf $C_{w}\in\mathcal{N}$ indexed by $w\in W_{{\mathbf{P}}}\backslash W_{\textup{aff}}/W_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ , it appears in ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})$ for some simple perverse sheaves $C_{1},C_{2}\in\mathcal{P}$ and some $i\neq 0$ . Since $C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2}$ is isomorphic to its own Verdier dual, we may assume $i>0$ . Using ( 4.3 ) and taking the top cohomology of $\textup{H}^{*}({{\mathbf{P}}_{0}/\mathbf{I}_{0}})$ , we conclude that $\pi^{\natural}{\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})$ is a direct summand of ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{a+i}(\pi^{\natural}C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle\mathbf{I}_{0}}}{{*}}\pi^{\natural}C_{2})(a/2)$ . Therefore $\pi^{\natural}C_{w}$ also appears in ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{a+i}(\pi^{\natural}C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle\mathbf{I}_{0}}}{{*}}\pi^{\natural}C_{2})(a/2)$ . Now $\pi^{\natural}C_{w}$ is $\textup{IC}_{\widetilde{w}}$ (up to a Tate twist) for $\widetilde{w}$ the longest representative of the coset $w$ . By the definition of the $a$ -function, this means $a(\widetilde{w})\geq a+i>a$ , hence $\widetilde{w}$ belongs to a smaller cell than $\underline{c}$ (we have a priori $\widetilde{w}\leq\underline{c}$ ). This shows that $\pi^{\natural}C_{w}\in\mathcal{Q}_{<\underline{c}}$ , hence $C_{w}\in\mathcal{P}_{<\underline{c}}$ . The conclusion is that $\mathcal{N}\subset\mathcal{P}_{<\underline{c}}$ and we have a Serre quotient functor $\overline{\mathcal{P}}\twoheadrightarrow\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ .

Taking $a$ -th perverse cohomologies of both sides of ( 4.3 ), we get 
 
 ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{a}(\pi^{\natural}C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle\mathbf{I}_{0}}}{{*}}\pi^{\natural}C_{2})\cong\bigoplus_{i\geq 0}\pi^{\natural}{\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{i}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})\otimes\textup{H}^{2a-i}({{\mathbf{P}}_{0}/\mathbf{I}_{0}})(a/2).$ 
 For $i>0$ the corresponding term on the right side lies in $\mathcal{N}$ , hence have zero image in $\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ by the previous discussion. Therefore $\pi^{\natural}C_{1}\odot\pi^{\natural}C_{2}$ , defined as the image of ${\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{a}(\pi^{\natural}C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle\mathbf{I}_{0}}}{{*}}\pi^{\natural}C_{2})(a/2)$ in $\mathcal{Q}_{\underline{c}}$ , is equal to the image of $\pi^{\natural}{\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{0}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})\otimes\textup{H}^{2a}({{\mathbf{P}}_{0}/\mathbf{I}_{0}})(a)\cong\pi^{\natural}{\vphantom{\textup{H}}}^{p}{\textup{H}}^{0}(C_{1}\stackrel{{\scriptstyle{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}{{*}}C_{2})$ in $\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ , which is the same as the image of $C_{1}\circledast C_{2}\in\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ in $\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ . This shows that $\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ is closed under $\odot$ , and that the quotient functor $\overline{\mathcal{P}}\to\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ is monoidal. ∎

4.16. Finish of the proof of Theorem 4.7

By Lemma 4.11 , $e$ is a t-exact functor, and therefore $e|_{\mathcal{P}}$ factors through the Serre quotient $\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ by the universal property of $\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ . We get a monoidal functor $\overline{e}:(\overline{\mathcal{P}},\circledast)\to(\textup{Vect},\otimes)$ .

Denote by $\bar{Z}$ the composition $\textup{Rep}(\widehat{G})\xrightarrow{Z_{{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}\mathcal{P}\twoheadrightarrow\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ , which is an exact monoidal functor. Let $\overline{\mathcal{P}}^{\prime}\subset\overline{\mathcal{P}}$ be the full subcategory consisting of subquotients of the images of $\bar{Z}$ . Similarly, let $\mathcal{P}^{\prime}_{\underline{c}}\subset\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}$ be the full subcategory consisting of subquotient of images of $\eta\circ\bar{Z}$ .

Summarizing, we have the following commutative diagram 
 
 $\textstyle{\textup{Rep}(\widehat{G})\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces}$ $\scriptstyle{\bar{Z}_{{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}}$ $\textstyle{\overline{\mathcal{P}}^{\prime}\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces}$ $\scriptstyle{\eta^{\prime}}$ $\textstyle{\overline{\mathcal{P}}\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces}$ $\scriptstyle{\eta}$ $\scriptstyle{\overline{e}}$ Vect $\textstyle{\mathcal{P}^{\prime}_{\underline{c}}\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces\ignorespaces}$ $\textstyle{\mathcal{P}_{\underline{c}}}$ 
 Both functors $\bar{Z}$ and $\eta^{\prime}\circ\bar{Z}$ are exact central functors. Applying a criterion of Bezrukavnikov in [1] to both functors, we see that both $\overline{\mathcal{P}}^{\prime}$ and $\mathcal{P}^{\prime}_{\underline{c}}$ are neutral Tannakian categories over $\overline{\mathbb{Q}}_{\ell}$ and hence the tensor functor $\eta^{\prime}$ between them must be faithful ( [4] ). However $\eta^{\prime}$ is also a Serre quotient functor. This forces $\eta^{\prime}$ to be an equivalence of tensor categories. Write $\overline{\mathcal{P}}^{\prime}\cong\textup{Rep}(\widehat{H})$ for some algebraic subgroup $\widehat{H}\subset\widehat{G}$ so that $\bar{Z}$ becomes the restriction functor from $\widehat{G}$ to $\widehat{H}$ . Therefore we may identify the two rows in the above diagram and obtain a factorization of the fiber functor $e\circ Z_{{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}$ as 
 
 $e\circ Z_{{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}:\textup{Rep}(\widehat{G})\xrightarrow{\textup{Res}^{\widehat{G}}_{\widehat{H}}}\textup{Rep}(\widehat{H})\cong\mathcal{P}^{\prime}_{\underline{c}}\cong\overline{\mathcal{P}}^{\prime}\xrightarrow{\overline{e}}\textup{Vect}.$

Fix a topological generator $\xi\in\mathcal{I}^{t}_{0}$ , then $\xi$ acts on the restriction functor $\textup{Res}^{\widehat{G}}_{\widehat{H}}$ . By Tannakian formalism, the action of $\xi$ on the fiber functor $\overline{e}\circ\textup{Res}^{\widehat{G}}_{\widehat{H}}=e\circ Z_{{\mathbf{P}}_{0}}$ gives an element $u\in\widehat{G}$ that commutes with $\widehat{H}$ (see [1] ). By ( 4.2 ), $u$ is conjugate to the image of $\xi$ under the monodromy representation of the local system $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ . Therefore we need to prove that $u\in\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ , i.e., $u$ corresponds to $\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ under Lusztig’s bijection in Theorem 4.2 . But this is exactly what was shown by Bezrukavnikov in [1] . This completes the proof of Theorem 4.7 .

5. Rigidity

In this section we shall deduce that $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ is cohomologically rigid under some additional assumptions. We work over the base field $\bar{k}$ without changing notation.

5.1. Cohomological rigidity

Recall that a $\widehat{G}$ -local system $E_{\widehat{G}}$ over an open subset $X^{\circ}$ of a complete smooth connected algebraic curve $X$ is called cohomologically rigid if 
 
 $\textup{H}^{*}({X,j_{!*}E^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G}}})=0$ 
 where $j:X^{\circ}\hookrightarrow X$ is the inclusion, $E^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G}}$ is the local system on $X^{\circ}$ associated with the adjoint representation of $\widehat{G}$ on its Lie algebra ${\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}$ , and $j_{!*}$ simply means the sheaf-theoretic direct image $\textup{H}^{0}j_{*}$ .

Back to the situation of § 2.6 with $\chi=1$ . We write $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(1,\phi)$ as $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ . Let $j:X^{\circ}\hookrightarrow X$ be the open inclusion, and $i_{0},i_{\infty}:\textup{Spec}\ \bar{k}\hookrightarrow X$ be the closed embedding of the two points $0$ and $\infty$ .

Assume $\chi=1$ and $G$ is split. Assume $\textup{char}(k)$ is sufficiently large so that $m=m({\mathbf{P}})$ is a regular elliptic number for $\mathbb{W}$ (see § 2.2 ). Assuming the properties (1) and (2) in § 2.5 about the monodromy of $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ at $\infty$ hold. Then we have 
 
 $\textup{H}^{*}({X,j_{!*}\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})=0.$ 
 I.e., $\textup{Kl}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ is cohomologically rigid.

Since ${\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{\mathcal{I}_{\infty}}=0$ by property (2) of § 2.5 , $\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ admits no global sections, i.e., $\textup{H}^{0}({X^{\circ},\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})=\textup{H}^{0}({X,j_{!*}\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})=0$ . Dually $\textup{H}_{c}^{2}({X^{\circ},\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})=\textup{H}^{2}({X,j_{!*}\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})=0$ .

The distinguished triangle $j_{!}\to j_{!*}\to i_{0,*}\textup{H}^{0}i^{*}_{0}\oplus i_{\infty,*}\textup{H}^{0}i_{\infty}^{*}\to j_{!}[1]$ gives an exact sequence 
 
 (5.1)  $0\to{\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{\mathcal{I}_{0}}\oplus{\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{\mathcal{I}_{\infty}}\to\textup{H}_{c}^{1}({X^{\circ},\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})\to\textup{H}^{1}({X,j_{!*}\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})\to 0.$ 
 The Grothendieck-Ogg-Shafarevich formula implies 
 
 $\chi_{c}(X^{\circ},\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi))=-\textup{Swan}_{\infty}({\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}).$ 
 Using the vanishing of $\textup{H}^{2}_{c}$ and property (2) of § 2.5 , we get 
 
 $\dim\textup{H}_{c}^{1}({X^{\circ},\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})=\#\Phi/m.$ 
 On the other hand, by Theorem 4.7 , ${\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{\mathcal{I}_{0}}$ is the dimension of the centralizer of a unipotent element $u$ in the unipotent conjugacy class $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ in $\widehat{G}$ . By Steinberg’s theorem, $\dim{\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{\mathcal{I}_{0}}=\dim{\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{u}=2\dim\mathcal{B}_{u}+r$ (where $r$ is the rank of $G$ ). By Theorem 4.2 (1), $\dim\mathcal{B}_{u}=a(\underline{c}_{{\mathbf{P}}})=\ell(w_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ . Hence $\dim\mathcal{B}_{u}=\#\Psi(L_{{\mathbf{P}}})/2$ ( $\Psi(L_{{\mathbf{P}}})$ is the root system of $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ ), and 
 
 $\dim{\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}}^{\mathcal{I}_{0}}=2\dim\mathcal{B}_{u}+r=\#\Psi(L_{{\mathbf{P}}})+r=\dim L_{{\mathbf{P}}}.$ 
 By the dictionary between stable gradings on Lie algebras and regular elements in Weyl groups given in [8] , $L_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is isomorphic to the neutral component of the fixed point subgroup of an automorphism of $\mathbb{G}$ given by a lifting of a regular elliptic element $w\in\mathbb{W}$ of order $m$ . By analyzing the action of $w$ on the Lie algebra of $\mathbb{G}$ it is easy to see that $\dim L_{{\mathbf{P}}}=\#\Phi/m$ . Therefore the first two terms in the exact sequence ( 5.1 ) both have dimension $\#\Phi/m$ , hence the third term vanishes. This proves the vanishing of $\textup{H}_{c}^{*}({X,j_{!*}\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)})$ in all degrees. ∎

When ${\mathbf{P}}=\mathbf{I}$ , we computed the Euler characteristic of $\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},\mathbf{I}}(\phi)$ in [10] and used this to confirm the predictions about the wild monodromy at $\infty$ in § 2.5 in the Iwahori case (in this case, these predictions were made in [9] ). However, the computation in [10] is very complicated (see the argument of Proposition 6.5 to get a sense). In principle, similar but even more complicated computation could be done for general admissible parahoric subgroups ${\mathbf{P}}$ , and the result would confirm the predictions in § 2.5 (at least when $\underline{u}_{{\mathbf{P}}}$ is a distinguished unipotent class, in which case $\textup{Kl}^{\textup{Ad}}_{\widehat{G},{\mathbf{P}}}(\phi)$ does not have global sections).

6. Examples: Quasi-split unitary groups

When $G$ is split of type $A$ , the only admissible standard parahoric subgroup is the Iwahori $\mathbf{I}$ . In this case, $\textup{Kl}_{{\vphantom{G}}^{L}{G},\mathbf{I}}(\chi,\phi)$ has been calculated in [10] , and is identified with the Kloosterman sheaves defined by Deligne. In section, we shall compute the generalized Kloosterman sheaves for the quasi-split unitary group $G$ .

6.1. Linear algebra

6.2. Stable locus

The dual space $V^{*}_{m}$ is the space of $\tau$ -invariant cyclic quivers of the shape ( 6.5 ), except that all arrows are reversed. Let $\phi_{i}:M_{i}\to M_{i+1}$ be the arrows. We think of $\phi_{\ell}:M_{\ell}\to M_{-\ell}$ as a quadratic form on $M_{\ell}$ . An element $\phi=(\phi_{-\ell},\cdots,\phi_{\ell})\in V^{*}_{m}$ is stable under the $L_{m}$ -action if and only if • All maps $\phi_{i}$ have the maximal possible rank; • We have two quadratic forms on $M_{0}$ : $q_{0}$ and the pullback of $\phi_{\ell}$ to $M_{0}$ via the map $\phi_{\ell-1}\cdots\phi_{0}:M_{0}\to M_{\ell}$ . We denote the second quadratic form by $q^{\prime}_{0}$ . Then these quadratic forms are in general position: the pencil of quadrics spanned by $q_{0}$ and $q^{\prime}_{0}$ is degenerate at $n$ distinct points on $\mathbb{P}^{1}$ . In particular, when $\dim M_{0}=d$ and $\phi$ is stable, then all $\phi_{i}$ are isomorphisms, and $\phi_{\ell}$ has at most a one-dimensional kernel. When $\dim M_{0}=d+1$ and $\phi$ is stable, then $\phi_{0}$ is surjective, $\phi_{1},\cdots,\phi_{\ell-1}$ are isomorphisms, and $\phi_{\ell}$ must be nondegenerate.

When $m=2$ , $V^{*}_{2}$ is simply the space of quadratic forms on $M$ . An element $\phi\in V^{*}_{2}(k)$ is stable under the $L_{2}=\textup{SO}(M,q)$ -action exactly when the quadratic form $\phi$ is in general position with $q$ (note $\phi$ itself can be degenerate).
